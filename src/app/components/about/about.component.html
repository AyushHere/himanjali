About Me

My journey into healing didn’t begin with textbooks, it began in my own body. I have lived with complex PTSD, and have battled many psychosomatic manifestations that included ,but were not limited to an autoimmune disorder , acne breakouts and burnout. It was through deep inner work, yoga , ayurvedic medicines and spiritual practices that I slowly found my way back to myself.

This is why I don’t believe in a one-size-fits-all method. Real healing is as unique as the person seeking it. Sometimes, it's a single word, a moment of presence, a revisit to an old memory, or a small action that brings you back to yourself after years spent in survival mode.

My academic background in BSc (Research) in Biotechnology and an MSc in Ayurveda Biology gave me structure and a scientific lens.. But it was self-inquiry, lived experience, and the integration of various healing modalities that truly transformed me.

Everything shifted in 2021 when I began working with yoga psychotherapist Ilashrei Anand. At a time when I had almost given up on healing, she introduced me to the profound depth of yoga psychology and met me with deep compassion. Her gentleness and intuitive presence helped me soften and become open to the possibility of healing.

It wasn’t easy. I struggled. I failed at many things, stuck in a freeze state, unable to fully show up, and feeling like I was constantly disappointing everyone around me. Over the past two years, I began taking my healing seriously. I held myself accountable , not out of shame, but from a place of love. That’s when things slowly began to shift. Gently, with time, I began to reclaim my life. Today, my work is deeply rooted in this lived experience. I support others on their healing journeys by weaving together scientific understanding, yogic wisdom, and spiritual inquiry. I collaborate with medical doctors, psychologists, and Ayurvedic Vaidyas to offer an integrative approach that honors the mind, body, and soul. Healing isn’t linear. It’s layered, deeply personal, and often messy. But it is possible. And if you’re reading this, I want you to know: it’s never too late to begin again.
